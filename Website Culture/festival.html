FESTIVAL



Luzon
Panagbenga Festival

Panagbenga is an annual flower festival celebrated every February which takes place in Baguio City, Philippines. The term “Panagbenga” comes from a Kankanaey term meaning “season of blooming”. This festival reflects the history, traditions and values of Baguio and the Cordilleras. It lasts over a month long and flourishes through community spirit involvement.

Pahiyas Festival

Every year on May 15, the Pahiyas Festival is held. Pahiyas Festival originated in the town of Lucban in the province of Quezon. The origins of the Pahiyas Festival can be traced all the way back to the 16th century. It wasn't like the festival we know now back then. Farmers would carry their harvests at the base of Mt. Banahaw and sell their wares. They gathered and celebrated humble feasts to thank the Anitos for their abundant harvests.

Visayas
Sinulog Festival

The Sinulog Festival, also known as the Santo Nino Festival, is a religious and cultural event held in Cebu every year. The event is regarded to be the first of the Philippines' most well-known festivals.

MassKara Festival

In the Philippines, the MassKara Festival is a hugely popular event. The name MassKara comes from the terms "mass," which means "many," and "kara," which means "faces," making it the Festival of Many Faces. In October, many people flock to Bacolod to experience this spectacular Filipino cultural celebration. Bacolod, nicknamed as the "City of Smiles," parades many smiling masks worn by inhabitants during this Philippine celebration. The large colorful masks are the focal point of the MassKara celebration. Many barangays and groups are putting in a lot of effort to put on world-class shows complete with handcrafted masks and eye-catching attire.

Mindanao
Kadayawan festival

In the Dabawnon language, kadayawan is a local expression. It comes from the Filipino word "madayaw," which means "good, valuable, or superior." The yearly Kadayawan is a thanksgiving event and a tribute to the indigenous people of Dabawe. This Philippines celebration takes place in the third week of August every year. As a symbol of respect for the fantastic year they'd had, people used to display fruits, vegetables, flowers, rice, and maize grains on their mats and in front of their dwellings. They used to sing and dance in the same way that they do now. (edited)

Kalinga Festival

KALILANGAN is Gensan's cultural festival, a celebration of unity in the midst of the city's ethnic diversity, known as Generals. With the colorful, dynamic, and mystical existence of its three peoples (Natives (Blaan's and T'boli's), Muslim Tribes, and the dominant Christian Groups), a festival was created to celebrate their coming together and promote peaceful coexistence. The fundamental feature of Kalilangan is that, unlike most other celebrations in the country, it is not religious. It is, nonetheless, a festival of unity, of Gensan culture, and of Gensan way of life
